Case Study: Mandeville Road Lining and Civil Works Project

Project Overview

The project, undertaken by McAllister, involved the installation of a lining system and combined civil works from concept design through to construction. Utilizing Building Information Modelling (BIM) processes and a digital strategy, we enhanced collaboration and cooperation with clients, supply chains, and stakeholders. The project required the production of an Environmental Management Plan and temporary work design and planning.

Challenges and Complexities

  • Multi-Track Rail Crossing: The project required navigating multi-track rail crossings, necessitating precise planning and coordination.
  • Syphon Crossing: Twin 750mm and 900mm syphon crossings under strategic rail lines with multiple bends posed significant challenges
  • Heavy Traffic and Load Management: Managing heavy traffic, rail, and construction phase loadings was critical to ensuring safety and structural integrity.
  • Limited Access Points: Limited and undersized asset access points required innovative solutions for effective execution.
  • Stakeholder Engagement: Engaging with multiple stakeholders and managing their expectations was crucial.
  • Site Access: Difficult site access and high volumes added to the project's complexity.

Solutions Implemented

To address these challenges, McAllister's internal design and modelling team explored all Cured-In-Place Pipe (CIPP) methods to develop a comprehensive solution:

  • Multi CIPP Solution: Implemented a solution with both Ultraviolet (UV) and composite felt lining to accommodate the complex bends and load requirements.
  • Scaffold Design and Erection: Designed, modeled, and erected multi-layered scaffolds to facilitate the lining solution.
  • Deconstruction and Reprofiling: Deconstructed original access points, including re-profiling and securing the rail verge.
  • New Manhole Construction: Constructed new manholes to incorporate a temporary flume system during the construction phase.
